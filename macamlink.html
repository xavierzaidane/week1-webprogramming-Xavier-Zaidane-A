He was known for his honesty, kindness, and compassion, and he was often sought out for his wisdom and guidance. As Muhammad grew older, he became increasingly troubled by the injustices and corruption that he saw around him. Gabriel revealed to him that he was the messenger of God, and that he had been chosen to guide humanity back to the true path. Over the next few years, Muhammad received revelations from God, which he shared with his family and friends. At first, his message was met with skepticism and even hostility, but gradually, a small group of followers began to gather around him. As the number of believers grew, Muhammad and his followers faced increasing persecution from the powerful leaders of Mecca. They were forced to leave their homes and seek refuge in the nearby city of Medina. In Medina, Muhammad established the first Islamic community, a model of social justice and equality. He instituted laws and customs based on the teachings of God, and he worked to build a society that was free from oppression and injustice.
